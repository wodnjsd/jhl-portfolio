A meditation pavilion commissioned by the local coastal trust, positioned at the threshold between managed farmland and a protected salt marsh nature reserve. The brief called for a structure that would provide shelter and orientation for visitors while leaving an absolutely minimal footprint on this sensitive landscape.

The building is raised on slender steel stilts, touching the ground at only eight points. The floor plate is clad in weathering steel grating that allows light and rain to pass through, and the roof — a single folded plane of standing-seam zinc — echoes the geometry of the dykes and drainage channels that grid the marsh below.

There are no walls. Instead, a series of pivoting timber fins, adjusted by the warden at the start of each season, modulate wind and provide dappled shade. The pavilion changes its character entirely with the light.
